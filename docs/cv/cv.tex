\section{Research} 


\marginnote{Awarded the Qualcomm Innovation Fellowship}

I am interested in automated reasoning, programming language theory, and formal
methods. Specifically, I am excited about useful formal languages that
theoretically and empirically lend themselves to efficient reasoning. All of my
publications---which are listed below---relate to this theme.

\section{Teaching and Mentoring}

\marginnote{Received UC Berkeley's Outstanding Graduate Student Peer Mentor and Teaching Assistant Awards}

My teaching and mentoring is primarily influenced by universal design for
learning (UDL) principles. I am particularly interested in how autograders can
maximize student autonomy and encourage active learning. I have taught at
the undergraduate and graduate levels and covered topics including programming
languages, formal methods, software engineering, and artificial intelligence.
